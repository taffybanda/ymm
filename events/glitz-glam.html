I think we often underestimate what it really means when people celebrate anniversaries. We see the beautiful decor, the congratulatory messages, the smiling photos, and the “I am so proud of you” moments. However, we rarely acknowledge the hard work behind it all and that is the sweat, the tears, the doubt, the anxiety, the moments of wanting to give up, and choosing to continue anyway.

So once again, allow me to say "2 years".

Young Moguls Mixer began as a conversation and just like many conversations these days, it could have easily ended there. But the founder, Nicole K. Duma chose to follow through. She took that conversation and transformed it into something tangible, and that is a space, a community, and a movement. Two years later, here we are celebrating the growth of Young Moguls.

On Saturday, 27 June, 2026, young moguls gathered together, bringing their glitz and glam to celebrate two years of Young Moguls and that is two years of vision, intention, and a dream brought to life.

The event was hosted by an incredible MC Miriro who set the tone for an evening that was filled with connection, reflection, and celebration.

One of the most memorable moments of the evening was hearing from the founder Nicole, herself as she took us through her journey. Among everything she shared, one message stood out, in spaces like these, what matters most is people showing up and that is showing up for opportunities, for growth, for community, and for each other.

The evening also gave us a chance to learn something unexpected and fun and that is dining etiquette. And believe it or not, there were moments where people were trying not to lose their composure after discovering that bread can actually be dipped in soup.

We then moved into Mogul Match, a networking segment that gave young moguls the opportunity to connect and learn more about one another professionally, creating meaningful conversations and opening doors to future collaborations.

The atmosphere was elevated by an amazing live band that brought energy and warmth to the celebration.

And then there was poetry.

A powerful performance by Fire Starter reminded everyone in the room why words matter. If you walked into Young Moguls Mixer feeling uncertain, overwhelmed, or unsure of your next step, Fire Starter became a voice of reflection and encouragement.

As the evening came to an end, Fire Starter left the room with a question that lingered long after the event ended:

“What must die so that what God placed inside of you may finally live?”

A powerful reminder that growth often requires release and that is letting go of fear, doubt, comfort, hesitation, or versions of ourselves that no longer serve where we are meant to go.

And perhaps that is something every Young Mogul should carry forward.

Two years later, Young Moguls Mixer is more than an event. It is proof that ideas followed by action can become communities, and dreams backed by consistency can become reality.

Here’s to celebrating the milestones people do not always see, and to many more years of Young Moguls.

By Ruth Tsanga
