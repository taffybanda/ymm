The Young Moguls Mixer: From Small Starts to Big Moves (Winter Edition) which was held on the 19th of July 2025 at Sabre Business World, was nothing short of extraordinary! This gathering of dynamic young minds, dreamers, and doers was an inspiring reminder that the future truly belongs to those who are ready to take small steps toward big moves.

The energy was unmatched, with a line-up of brilliant speakers, Mr G. Viki, Dr H. Mhondiwa, and Mr S. Mushakwe who each brought fresh insights that left everyone in the room feeling motivated and empowered.

Mr Viki’s session was a masterclass on financial wisdom. He reminded us that building wealth begins with discipline, determination, and diligence. In his words, “You can save and not touch that money you put under your pillow.” His talk challenged us to unlearn bad spending habits that hinder us from saving, rethink our financial preferences (yes, even the temptation of that brand-new I-phone, you’ve been eyeing), and adopt a savings culture. His message was crystal clear, “As long as you have income, whether it is a job or even pocket money, you can save.”

Dr Mhondiwa captivated the audience with her focus on the power of networking. She shared practical tips on building and maintaining meaningful relationships that open doors to both personal and professional opportunities. Her session was interactive, with thought-provoking answers to questions that emphasised why your network truly determines your net worth.

When it comes to building long-term wealth, Mr Mushakwe made it clear that real estate is still king. He encouraged us as young people to think strategically about where to invest, revealing that high-density areas often offer better returns on rental properties compared to low-density suburbs. His session left the crowd inspired to start looking at property investment through a more analytical and opportunity-driven lens.

The day was not just about ideas. It was also about celebrating young talent. Electrifying performances from Mitchell Duma - CXXVII set the room ablaze with creativity, rhythm, and pure energy. These performances were a reminder that young people are not just chasing opportunities. We are creating waves and setting the world on fire.

The Young Moguls Mixer is more than an event. In the words of one of the attendants Mr T. Dzingayi - "it is a great community." It is a family of visionaries who believe in growth, collaboration, and turning dreams into reality. Being part of this movement means surrounding yourself with people who push you to do better, dream bigger, and act smarter.

And if you could not make it this time? It is never too late. The next Young Moguls Mixer is waiting for you, and we cannot wait to welcome you into the family.

From small starts to big moves. This is only the beginning!!!!!

By Ruth Tsanga
