We are often told that passion alone is not enough especially if it does not pay. And for many young people, that tension has led to the quiet abandonment of what they truly love in pursuit of financial security. But what if you did not have to choose?

This year’s Young Moguls Mixer challenged that narrative with a powerful theme: From Passion to Paycheck - Earn From What You Love. Held on Saturday, 21 February 2026, the event brought together a dynamic community of young leaders, creatives, and aspiring entrepreneurs for an experience that was both inspiring and practical.

The day kicked off with insights from Tinaye Makoni, who unpacked the essence of entrepreneurship as “the pursuit of opportunity without regard to resources currently controlled.” His session on "Startup Funding and Finance" pushed attendees to think beyond limitations, emphasising that opportunity lies in identifying a product or service that can generate value for all stakeholders. A standout moment was the reminder that even global giants like Samsung had humble beginnings - starting in entirely different industries before evolving into what we know today. Through the 6-WH opportunity assessment framework, participants were challenged to interrogate their why, what, who, where, when, and how, while also reflecting on funding options, adaptability, and the importance of not being confined to a single idea.

Noxy Duri then took the conversation deeper, bridging the gap between passion and profitability in her session "Turning Passion Into Purpose". Her message was clear: passion is not a license for complacency and laziness. Without structure, discipline, and intentionality, passion can lead to scattered efforts rather than meaningful progress. She emphasised that a paycheck demands stewardship of time, skills, and opportunities. In a world driven by visibility and digital presence, she challenged young people to move beyond “vibes” and take themselves seriously. Skill alone is not enough. Voice, clarity, and consistency matter. Her guiding questions left the audience with practical tools for reflection:

What is my vision in one sentence?

What season am I in?

What visible action can I take?

The final session, led by Kudakwashe Gumbie, demystified the world of investment. Moving beyond fear and excitement, he grounded the discussion in reality: investors are not just buying ideas. They are buying reduced risk. Participants gained insight into the key factors investors consider, including the strength of the idea, the team behind it, financials, traction, growth potential, and the certainty of returns. Through frameworks like market pull and unit economics, the session equipped young entrepreneurs with the tools to think strategically and present ventures that inspire confidence.

And, as always, the Young Moguls Mixer would not be complete without a vibrant creative close, featuring an electrifying performance by CXXVII featuring Absolom Chikwezvero which was a reminder that passion, when nurtured with intention, can indeed become both purpose and profession.

From thought-provoking discussions to actionable insights, this year’s Mixer was more than just an event. It was a call to action. A call for young people to align passion with purpose, strategy with creativity, and ultimately, dreams with sustainable income.

By Ruth Tsanga
